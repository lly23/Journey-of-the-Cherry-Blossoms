Journey of the Cherry Blossoms

Cherry Blossoms, known as “Sakura” in Japanese, originate from the earths of Japan also representing the country as the national flower. However, every spring, you can find cherry blossom trees coming to a full bloom in many areas of the U.S. Their trek to the other side of the world

1885

  • Eliza Ruhamah Scidmore, a photographer and geographer on the board of the National Geographic Society, returns from her visit to Japan. Upon her arrival she immediately proposes the planting of Cherry Blossoms to government officials but was turned down.

1906

  • David Fairchild, a plant explorer and U.S. Department of Agriculture official, imports 75 flowering cherry trees from the Yokohama Nursery Co. in Japan. He plants them on his land in Chevy Chase to test their hardiness.
